Course Description
Students in this course tackle real-world biomedical engineering challenges. Projects may involve designing medical devices, developing diagnostic tools, or creating assistive technologies. The course emphasizes interdisciplinary collaboration and user-centered design.
Learning Outcomes
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
1.
Conduct needs assessment and problem definition for biomedical engineering projects.
2.
Generate and select design concepts based on user needs.
3.
Develop detailed designs and prototypes for biomedical applications.
4.
Perform clinical testing and validation of biomedical devices.
5.
Understand regulatory considerations for biomedical engineering.
